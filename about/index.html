who is spencer?

programmer, artist, writer, internet imagineer, computational poet

Spencer creates infrastructure for a more playful, light, and communal internet in the form of new interaction mediums, website environments, open protocols, and local-first applications. He does this through creating (tools|games|spaces) that push how we’re allowed to dictate digital places and objects, making site-specific net art that question our assumptions of what websites are and who we are when we visit them, and writing about his findings and the emotional journey of this independent path.

They find it hard to describe themselves, so they prefer to reveal who they are through the things they care about. They’ve designed and built products at Square, Airbnb, and most recently Coda and verses. Their art has been shown at the de Young Museum, CultureHub, and SFPC, and their work has been featured in Frieze and MIT Technology Review. Their words have been published in Kernel Magazine, Syllabus Project, and regularly in their newsletter spencer’s paradoxes.

Some other explorations that don’t quite fit into these boxes include baristaing, site-specific digital media installations, unusual mediums for computing, and communal sites for connection.

I write for emotional resonance and thought provoking, so please message me (spencerc99@gmail.com) if you have thoughts :)
